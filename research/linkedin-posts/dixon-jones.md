We've been measuring the wrong thing for AI search.

For thirty years, SEO has been about ranking pages. That mental model still holds in Google. But AI recommendation engines don't rank pages. They build associations. And those are not the same investment.

Here is a metric our industry needs: AI Topical Presence. It measures how strongly, and how broadly, an AI model connects a brand to the commercial topics in its market. Not whether you appear in AI answers, but WHAT you appear for.

This is a Waikay AI Competitive Map for the query "What are the best semantic SEO tools", and it tells a much more interesting story than Share of Voice alone.

- Ahrefs has a Topical Presence score of around 60, but sits below 5% AI Share of Voice. It is a Hidden Expert: the AI knows Ahrefs well, connects it to the right topics, but doesn't mention it as often as the leaders.
- inLinks sits just above the mid-tier threshold on Topical Presence, with reasonable Share of Voice. Room to grow, but solid foundations.
- The bottom right quadrant is labelled "Hollow Presence". High Share of Voice, low Topical Presence. The AI mentions the brand often, but the associations are shallow. That brand is one model update away from a significant drop.

Topical Presence has three components:

- **Depth:** how strongly the AI connects your brand to the topics that matter
- **Breadth:** how many of those core topics your brand covers at all
- **Concentration:** whether your visibility depends on just one or two topics, or is genuinely distributed

The combination tells you which lever to pull. A brand weak on Breadth needs to build new territory. A brand weak on Depth needs to strengthen what it already covers. A brand with high Concentration needs to diversify before the next model update does it for them.

And when a gap surfaces, the first question should not be "what should we write?" It should be: is this a content gap, a documentation gap, or a product gap? Each answer leads somewhere different.
